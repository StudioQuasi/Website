Water Futures
Exhibition Installation

Water Futures was a year-long research program and design competition confronting the global drinking water crisis. Hosted by A/D/O and curated by Jane Withers studio, the program concluded with an exhibition showcasing the work of 9 finalists.

Quasi was commissioned to develop a modular exhibition system to present this multi-media work. The objective was to create a space within A/D/O that gave focus to the work through a consistent design language, but also live cohesively in the large open space of A/D/O.

Our approach was to use readily available base materials, with a preference to sustainability. We selected 4” cardboard shipping tubes as our building block: they create volume and structure with a relatively small amount of material.

Ideally, the exhibit could be rebuilt anywhere in the world by shipping key structural components and sourcing the structural tubes locally.

Using a primary diameter of 11 feet and a consistent tube spacing of ¾”, we created a set of support ribs, tables and picture mounts that could be mixed and matched to dynamically change the exhibit program as we built it out.

A system of pinching tubes between two rails creates a secure fit without drilling or cutting into the cardboard. This leaves them intact for reuse.
